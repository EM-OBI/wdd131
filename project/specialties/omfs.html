What are the basic technical considerations in performing an incision?

  • Use a sharp blade of appropriate size.
  • A firm, continuous stroke is preferable to short, soft, repeated strokes.
  • Avoid vital structures.
  • Use incisions that are parallel to relaxed skin tension lines.
  • Consider the anatomy of the site in placement of the incision.

For making an incision in an epithelial surface, how should the scalpel blade be oriented?

To avoid bias, the incision should be made parallel to the epithelial surface.

What factors influence the placement of incisions in the mouth?

  • Anatomy and location of vital structures
  • Convenience and access

What are the principles of flap design?

  • Flap design should ensure adequate blood supply; the base of the flap should be larger than the apex.
  • Reflection of the flap should adequately expose the operative field.
  • Flap design should permit atraumatic closure of the wound.

What are the most frequent causes of the tearing of mucogingival flaps?

  1. Flaps are too small to provide adequate exposure.
  2. Too much force is used to elevate the flaps.
  3. The flap is not being elevated in the subperiosteal plane
